# Business Impact Model: NexusGraph

To quantify the value of NexusGraph, we isolate a typical Tier-1 automotive supply chain scenario.

## Baseline Assumptions
- **Supply Chain Revenue Value:** $100,000,000 / year ($100M)
- **Daily Revenue Generation:** ~$273,972 / day (assuming 365 day sustained operation)
- **Hourly Downtime Cost:** ~$11,415 / hour

## The Scenario
A critical global distribution center (DC1) unexpectedly ceases operations due to a massive port strike. The supply chain has identical inventory at DC2 but must manually reroute transport logistics.

### Traditional Response (Manual Re-routing)
- Information lag, meeting coordination, and spreadsheet-based capacity math.
- **Average Time to Resolution:** 4 hours.
- **Total Revenue Suppressed:** 4 hours * $11,415 = **$45,660 loss.**

### NexusGraph Autonomous Response
- The Signal Agent detects the disruption alert instantly.
- The Graph Engine isolates DC1, maps cascade survivability, and triggers rerouting to DC2.
- **Average Time to Resolution:** ~3 seconds.
- **Total Revenue Suppressed:** ~0 hours (Actioned before active manufacturing buffer depletion).

## Annualized Return on Investment (ROI)
Assuming a highly optimized global network faces roughly 12 major topological disruptions annually:
- **Traditional Loss:** $45,660 * 12 = **$547,920**
- **NexusGraph Impact:** Limits the delay factor to zero downtime, directly recovering **~$500K+ in protected revenue per $100M of chain.**

NexusGraph fundamentally changes disruption management from reactive damage control to proactive mathematical optimization.
